1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document is to provide a comprehensive description of the Health-Sync system. It includes both functional and non-functional requirements, detailing the intended functionality, performance, and constraints of the system. This document serves as a guide for the development, testing, and maintenance of Health-Sync.

1.2 Scope

Health-Sync is a web-based application designed to facilitate the management of medical records and the interaction between patients and healthcare providers. The primary features include user registration, doctor recommendations, medical record management, and secure communication between patients and doctors.

Overall Description 2.1 Product Perspective Health-Sync is a standalone web application designed to provide users with a seamless experience in managing their health records and interacting with healthcare providers. It integrates with third-party services for enhanced functionality, such as telehealth platforms and payment gateways. 2.2 Product Functions User Registration and Authentication Doctor Recommendations Medical Record Management Document Upload Patient-Doctor Communication Role-Based Access Control Admin Approval for Doctors 2.3 User Characteristics Patients: Individuals seeking to manage their health records and consult with doctors. Doctors: Healthcare providers who manage patient profiles and provide consultations. Admin: Users responsible for approving doctor registrations and maintaining the system. 2.4 Constraints The system must comply with HIPAA and GDPR regulations. The system must be accessible according to WCAG 2.1 AA standards. Data must be encrypted in transit and at rest. 2.5 Assumptions and Dependencies Users have access to the internet and a web browser. Third-party services for payment processing and telehealth are reliable and accessible. 3. Specific Requirements 3.1 Functional Requirements 3.1.1 User Registration and Authentication FR1.1: The system shall allow users to register as a patient or doctor. FR1.2: The system shall authenticate users via username and password. FR1.3: The system shall implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all users. 3.1.2 Doctor Recommendations FR2.1: The system shall recommend doctors based on user preferences and history. FR2.2: The system shall display a list of doctors with their details and ratings. 3.1.3 Medical Record Management FR3.1: The system shall allow patients to create and manage their medical records. FR3.2: The system shall allow patients to upload and associate documents with their medical records. FR3.3: The system shall allow doctors to view and update patient medical records. 3.1.4 Patient-Doctor Communication FR4.1: The system shall allow patients to add doctors to their profile. FR4.2: The system shall allow doctors to comment on patient records. FR4.3: The system shall facilitate secure communication between patients and doctors. 3.1.5 Admin Approval for Doctors FR5.1: The system shall allow admins to review and approve doctor registrations. FR5.2: The system shall notify doctors of their approval status. 3.2 Non-Functional Requirements 3.2.1 Performance NFR1.1: The system shall respond to user actions within 2 seconds for 95% of interactions. NFR1.2: The system shall handle up to 10,000 concurrent users. NFR1.3: The system shall support up to 1,000 transactions per second during peak times. 3.2.2 Security NFR2.1: The system shall encrypt all data in transit using SSL/TLS and at rest using AES-256 encryption. NFR2.2: The system shall implement role-based access control (RBAC). NFR2.3: The system shall ensure data integrity using checksums or hash functions. 3.2.3 Usability NFR3.1: The user interface shall be intuitive and easy to navigate. NFR3.2: The system shall comply with WCAG 2.1 AA standards. NFR3.3: The system shall provide comprehensive help resources and user documentation. 3.2.4 Reliability NFR4.1: The system shall maintain an uptime of 99.9%. NFR4.2: The system shall be designed to tolerate hardware and software failures. NFR4.3: The system shall perform daily backups and provide data recovery within 4 hours. 3.2.5 Maintainability NFR5.1: The system shall be designed with a modular architecture. NFR5.2: The system shall include comprehensive technical documentation. NFR5.3: The system shall implement robust error logging and monitoring. 3.2.6 Interoperability NFR6.1: The system shall provide well-documented RESTful APIs for integration. NFR6.2: The system shall use standard data formats and protocols (e.g., HL7, FHIR). NFR6.3: The system shall support integrations with popular third-party services. 3.2.7 Compliance NFR7.1: The system shall comply with HIPAA regulations for users in the United States. NFR7.2: The system shall comply with GDPR regulations for users in the European Union. NFR7.3: The system shall implement data retention policies in compliance with relevant regulations. 4. Appendices 4.1 Appendix A: Glossary Health-Sync: The web-based application designed for managing medical records and facilitating patient-doctor interactions. PHI: Personal Health Information, which includes any information about health status, provision of healthcare, or payment for healthcare that can be linked to an individual. HIPAA: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, a U.S. law that sets standards for the protection of sensitive patient data. GDPR: General Data Protection Regulation, a regulation in EU law that governs data protection and privacy for individuals within the European Union. WCAG: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines, which provide guidelines for making web content more accessible to people with disabilities. SSL/TLS: Secure Sockets Layer / Transport Layer Security, protocols for encrypting data transmitted over the internet. MFA: Multi-Factor Authentication, a security process that requires more than one method of authentication to verify a user's identity. RBAC: Role-Based Access Control, a system of managing user permissions based on their role within an organization.
